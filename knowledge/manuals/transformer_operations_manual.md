# Transformer Operations Manual

## Thermal Overload Response

When transformer oil temperature approaches 80 C, increase monitoring frequency and confirm that
cooling fans, pumps, and radiator airflow are operating normally. Operators should prepare a
controlled load reduction plan before the transformer reaches emergency limits.

When transformer oil temperature reaches or exceeds 90 C, the event must be treated as a thermal
overload. Operations should initiate controlled load shedding, keep protective alarms and
interlocks enabled, notify the duty engineer, and record the event in the operating log.

## Load Limits

Normal continuous operation should remain at or below 100% of rated load unless a site-specific
engineering procedure authorizes temporary overload. Emergency operation above 120% of rated load
is not permitted in this demo environment. Agents and operators must not bypass safety alarms to
continue overload operation.

## Voltage And Frequency Envelope

Voltage should remain within +/-5% of nominal voltage. Frequency should remain between 59.7 Hz and
60.3 Hz for stable operation. Sustained deviations require operator notification and root-cause
analysis.
